How a mover gets onto TruMove

TruMove does not just add every carrier that knocks on the door. Before a mover is allowed on the platform, they go through a multi step review that looks at who they are, how they operate, and how they have treated customers in the past.

Once approved, they are monitored on every job. If their performance slips or risk signals pop up, they are pushed down in results or removed entirely. Our AI models and ops team work together to keep bad actors away from your move.

1
Pre screen
Business identity, years in business, service areas, and ownership verified.
2
Compliance

3
Reputation
Reviews, complaints, and claims history screened across multiple sources.
4
Live scoring
On time rate, damages, and upcharges tracked on every TruMove job.

Zero tolerance for bad behavior.

TruMove has written rules that every carrier agrees to before they ever see a job. Certain behavior gets an immediate suspension or permanent removal from the platform.

  • Hostage loads or extortion ‚Äì demanding more money after loading or refusing delivery.
  • Systematic bait and switch pricing ‚Äì large, unjustified increases far beyond the quoted range.
  • Serious safety violations ‚Äì unqualified drivers, blatant hours of service abuse, or unsafe vehicles.
  • Fraudulent reviews or identities ‚Äì fake review farms, shell companies, or repeated reincarnations.
  • Chronic damage and unpaid claims ‚Äì patterns that show customers consistently being left unprotected.
